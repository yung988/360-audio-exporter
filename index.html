Explore the Core Workflows

Orbit 360 operates in two primary modes designed to address the main pain points of 360° VR content creation.

Convert 360° Video

Transcode camera footage and master files with extensive control over video encoding, resolution scaling, frame rates, and audio mapping.

  • Video Codecs: Hardware-accelerated H.264 (VideoToolbox), HEVC (VideoToolbox), ProRes 422, VP9, or copy directly without re-encoding.
  • Scaling & Resolution: Same as source, 8K (7680x3840), 5.7K (5760x2880), 5K, 4K, 3K, 2K, or custom resolutions.
  • Audio Handling: Downmix multi-channel feeds to Stereo (AAC), preserve spatial formats (AAC at up to 768 kbps), copy original raw audio tracks, or remove audio completely.
  • Framerate Control: Keep source rates or target film-standard 23.98 fps, 29.97 fps, 50 fps, or 60 fps.

Audio Transfer

Copy audio from a source file into an existing video export. Useful when you already have a finished video file and want to replace or add the correct audio track (stereo, original camera audio, multi-channel, or spatial) without re-encoding the video when possible.

  • 1:1 Video Stream Copy: The finished, graded video is copied directly without re-encoding—preserving 100% of the video quality and completing in seconds.
  • Diverse Audio Formats: Import multichannel fields, standard stereo, mono, or multi-track feeds directly from camera files, recorders, or external WAV/AAC masters.
  • Flexible Mapping: Choose to replace the existing video stream's audio, or keep it and pair it with the new track.
  • VR Tags Intact: Existing equirectangular metadata is automatically detected and preserved.

Instant VR Validation

Don't waste hours uploading bad files. When a job finishes, Orbit 360 immediately probes the output file and displays a post-export checklist to guarantee full VR compatibility.

  • Checks projection metadata: Verifies correct "equirectangular" tagging for VR players.
  • Checks audio stream properties: Audits channel count and maps the layout.
  • Confirms file containers and stream alignments.
  • Warns of potential configuration errors immediately in the UI.

How Orbit 360 Handles Audio Streams

When editing or grading 360° videos in editing software, spatial audio configuration or multi-track details can easily be lost or downmixed on export. Orbit 360 lets you select the finished video and an audio source containing the original tracks to merge them together.

Our utility uses ffprobe to analyze audio configurations. It copy-pastes the video track 1:1 to preserve the original master encoding, merges the selected audio stream, and preserves spherical/360° metadata for correct playback in VR players.

Audio Transfer checklist:

  • Choose the finished master video as the main input.
  • Choose the camera file, WAV, or AAC containing your target audio.
  • Select whether to replace or append the audio track.
  • Run the transfer to create a copy without video re-encoding.

# Compile universal app bundles
